Panthers Forward FAQ



 If you have questions on the Panthers Forward program, please see the FAQs below. If you still have questions, contact us.


 About the Program

What is Panthers Forward?
Panthers Forward is a new model for reducing the burden of student debt while supporting student success. Launched by the University of Pittsburgh Office of the Chancellor, this model involves reducing a student’s federal loan balance and connecting each participant with a supportive professional development network. In return, participants are expected to pay it forward after graduation so that other students have an opportunity to receive the same level of support.
How does the program help the student debt crisis?
Current students have very few options, outside of the traditional student loan structure, to finance their advanced degree. Panthers Forward gives these students a new, individualized and interest-free option for reducing the burden of student loans.
How does Pitt benefit?
Pitt does not directly benefit or profit from Panthers Forward. At the same time, the university, City of Pittsburgh and general economy benefit, in numerous ways, when Pitt students and alumni have more financial freedom and stability.
What is the Panthers Forward pledge?
The Panthers Forward pledge is a non-contractual promise to contribute both money and time in a manner that helps sustain the program and offer equivalent opportunities to future participants.

 Application Questons

How do I apply?
The Panthers Forward application is online and should take less than 20 minutes to complete. Applying does not guarantee admission into the program.
Am I eligible to apply for the program if I do not have student loans?
No. Participants must have taken out federal student loans to be eligible for the program.
Am I eligible for Panthers Forward if I am a part-time student?
No. At this time, only full-time students can apply to the program.
I attend a University of Pittsburgh regional campus. Am I eligible to apply?
Not at this time.
What is the application deadline?
The application is due by 11:59PM EST on Wednesday, Oct. 31, and should less than 20 minutes to complete. Apply Here.
What qualities are you looking for in an applicant?
Eligible Panthers Forward participants must be:
  • On track to graduate in the spring of 2019.
  • Have received up to $5,000 in federal loan support during their senior year.
  • Full-time students at the university’s Pittsburgh campus.
  • In good standing with the university.
  • Active and engaged members of the Pitt community.
What is your application review process like?
We are utilizing a holistic review process that is similar to the process employed by the University’s Office of Admissions and Financial Aid. As part of this process, two separate committees, composed of staff and faculty members, will review each application and contribute to its final score.

 Student Participation Questions

What are students pledging to do?
Participants are pledging to provide financial and community support to future Panthers Forward participants.
When will we know who made it into the program?
We hope to announce awardees on Monday, Nov. 26.
If I am selected, how do I get my grant?
The University will pay up to $5,000 of a participating student’s federal loans. This payment, which will go directly to the student’s federal loan service provider, will be disbursed shortly after they graduate from Pitt.
Can I be awarded more than one Panthers Forward grant?
No. Students can only receive one Panthers Forward grant at this time.
If I am accepted into the program, what kind of commitment am I making?
Panthers Forward participants will be asked to pay it forward to sustain the program for future Pitt students. This commitment includes pledging to follow an individualized, interest-free donation plan. In addition, participants will have an opportunity to attend several mentorship and networking events at the university.
How do Panthers Forward alumni and students interact?
Every relationship is unique, but Panthers Forward participants will have a chance to discuss career goals, challenges and opportunities with Alumni Ambassador mentors and other alumni. To facilitate these discussions, the university will host various events throughout the year, such as one-on-one coffee chats and larger networking events.
When do I have to start paying it forward?
We ask participants to begin making monthly donations once they graduate from Pitt.
What happens if I receive a grant but cannot pay it forward into the program after graduating from Pitt?
Sustaining the program relies on participants honoring their monthly pledge. However, members of Panthers Forward can and should modify their monthly donation total to better align with their changing financial needs.
How long am I a part of this program?
Once accepted into the program, Panthers Forward participants are lifelong members.

 Getting Involved

I am a Pitt alum. How can I get involved?
Any alumni interested in learning about opportunities to get involved in Panthers Forward can contact us.
What is the alumni ambassador team?
As part of Panthers Forward, we have created an exclusive Alumni Ambassador team to connect with participants and to support their professional success. These team members will share their experiences, perspectives and advice with students in the program. If you are interested in becoming involved as an alumni in Panthers Forward, please contact us.
