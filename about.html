About Panthers Forward

Program Summary

Student loan debt is rising—and carries lifelong consequences. It’s a problem with no easy solution. That’s why we’re solving it on our own, and we’re doing it in classic Pitt Panther style: Together.

Introducing Panthers Forward, an innovative program—unique to the University of Pittsburgh—that leverages the power of our university community to ease the burden of student loan debt.

The first Panthers Forward class will graduate from the Pittsburgh campus in spring 2019. To be eligible, these students must be accepted into the program and have taken out federal loans to fund their senior year of classes. Upon graduation, Panthers Forward participants will receive up to $5,000 in direct federal student loan relief. The only condition is a voluntary one: That they pay-it-forward. There is no fixed repayment plan or third-party broker. It's just Pitt graduates helping Pitt students succeed.

 How it Works

  • Eligible students must be in good academic standing and have received federal student loans during the 2018-2019 school year.
  • Panthers Forward participants will also receive access to an exclusive network of alumni-mentors who are dedicated to supporting their success.
  • Upon graduation, Panthers Forward participants are asked to pay it forward to future students.
  • Participants are under no contractual obligation to repay, but their support is necessary to sustain the program.
  • Donations are tax deductible and directly support student success and student debt relief.

Program Highlights


Paying it Forward

Upon graduation, Panthers Forward participants are asked to give back to help support students following in their path. Their support directly helps lower student loan debt—and boost financial freedom—for future generations of Panthers.

A New Model

No interest rates. No one-size-fits-all repayment plan. No third-party companies. Panthers Forward, an initiative launched by Chancellor Patrick Gallagher, gives Pitt students the freedom and flexibility to give back on their terms and give directly to current students in need.

Panther Pride

The program's success hinges on meaningful mentorship and networking opportunities and the power of the Pitt community. Panthers Forward participants forge strong ties to Pitt alumni who share their career interests and goals.

A Financial Burden—Lightened

Every inaugural Panthers Forward participant will have their federal student loans cut by up to $5,000 at the end of their senior year.
