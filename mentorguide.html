Panthers Forward Class of 2020 Mentor Expectations

Overview

Launched in 2018, Panthers Forward is an innovative program that addresses student debt via a pay-it-forward model. The program provides participating Pitt seniors with up to $5,000 each, which is applied directly to their federal student loan balance upon graduation, thereby reducing their overall debt total. Once young alumni, participants will be asked to “pay it forward” directly to the Panthers Forward program in the form of contributions and in time increments of their choosing. These funds will be used to support successive Panthers Forward students.

As a member of the Panthers Forward Mentor Committee, you will play an important role in strengthening the professional development and networking aspects of the Panthers Forward program. Mentoring in this program is intended to be flexible, yet structured. Mentorship will not focus on specific careers, but rather on life skills – skills that all adults need to learn in order to strengthen their personal and professional success.

Who is involved in the program?

You and your fellow 2020 Panthers Forward Mentor Committee members. This is a group of approximately 35-40 Pitt alumni, whose experience spans a range of class years, majors and careers, and are dedicated to providing a professional network and guidance to Panthers Forward students.

Panthers Forward participating students. This is a group of 150 Pittsburgh Campus undergraduate seniors who have federal student loan debt and will graduate in 2020.

Program Kickoff: Welcome Celebration during Homecoming Weekend
To start the year off right, there will be an afternoon reception on Friday, October 25th, 4 - 6 p.m. at The Oaklander Hotel to bring together the 2020 class of Panthers Forward participating students, the Panthers Forward Mentor Committee and the 2019 Panthers Forward alumni to celebrate the start of the second year of the program. Mentoring Communities
Each member of the Panthers Forward Mentor Committee will oversee a mentoring community made up of four to five Panthers Forward participating students. These students are grouped according to the life skill interests expressed in the students’ and mentors’ applications. Mentors are expected to meet and/or connect with the students in their mentoring community to offer expertise, guidance, and support regarding students’ post-graduation plans. These communities will be announced shortly after the welcome celebration and run through the end of April. Mentoring communities will be provided with monthly touch point topics to guide communication between students and mentors. While students are expected to contact their alumni mentor a minimum of once per month to fulfill these touch point expectations, mentors are also expected to remain proactively in touch with their assigned mentees. Please think through how you can best communicate with your assigned students monthly. Communication can be done in a group setting, individually, in person, or virtually.

In addition to connecting with assigned mentees, mentors can connect with other Panthers Forward participating students (and fellow mentors) throughout the academic year. More information on this is mentioned in the Staying Connected section found below.
Life Skills Symposia
Three symposia, each featuring one of the life skills covered by the program, will be held over the course of the academic year. These events are intended to be fun, interactive and engaging, with a keynote speaker providing special insight into the symposium topic, Q&A and a networking reception with refreshments. Symposia will take place off-campus. The symposium topics and dates are:
  • How to Network & Why It's Important: Monday, November 18, 5 - 7 p.m.
  • Financial Literacy: Monday, January 27, 5 - 7 p.m.
  • The Power of Giving: Monday, March 30, 5 - 7 p.m.
Additional information, including location and keynote speaker related to these symposia, will be provided in advance of each event. If you are interested in hosting or speaking please contact the Panthers Forward Office.
Staying Connected
You will receive an email with instructions to join a private Panthers Forward group on LinkedIn. We are asking the entire Panthers Forward community to join this group in order to better facilitate networking and discussion amongst mentors and students, as well as across mentoring community groups. Panthers Forward participants should use this group to connect, ask questions, brainstorm, share experiences, and support one another throughout the academic year. The Panthers Forward staff will also use this platform to encourage touch point discussions and share information about upcoming program-related events.
